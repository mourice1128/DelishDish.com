America's Classic No-Fail Chocolate Chip Cookies
  • 2¼ Cups Flour
  • 1 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1 Cup Butter, Melted Then Cooled
  • 1 Cup Sugar
  • ½ Cup Brown Sugar
  • 2 Eggs, Slightly Beaten
  • 2 Cups Semisweet Chocolate Chips
  1. Preheat oven to 375°
  2. Combine flour, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl; set aside.
  3. Beat butter, sugar, brown sugar by hand in large bowl, until combined.
  4. Add eggs and vanilla; beat until well blended.
  5. Add flour mixture, mix until combined.
  6. Stir in chocolate chips.
  7. Drop cookie dough by rounded tbsp onto ungreased cookie sheet about 1"- 1½" apart.
  8. Bake 12-14 minutes or until edges are crisp, but centers are still soft.
  9. Let stand on cookie sheet about 2 minutes on cookie sheet prior to moving to wire cooling rack.

Soft Cut-Out Sugar Cookies
  • ¾ Unsalted Butter, Sofened to Room Temp
  • ¾ Cup Sugar
  • 1 Lg. Egg, Room Temp
  • 2 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • ¼ tsp Almond Extract
  • 2¼ Flour
  • ½ tsp Baking Powder
  • ¼ tsp
  1. In lg bowl, using the paddle mixer attachment, beat butter until smooth; about 1 minute.
  2. Add sugar and beat on high speed until light and fluffy; about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add the egg, vanilla, and almond extract; beat on high until fully mixed (about 2 mins.)
    4. ► Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed. ◄
  4. Whisk the flour, baking powder, and salt together in a medium bowl.
  5. Turn mixer to low and add about ½ of the flour mixture, beating until barely combined.
    6. ► If it is too soft, add 1 Tbsp more of flour until better consistency. ◄
  6. Divide dough into 2 equal parts.
  7. Roll each portion out onto a piece of parchment paper about ¼" thick.
  8. Stack the pieces (with paper) onto a baking sheet; refridgerate at least 1 hr.
    9. ► Chilling is mandatory; If longer than 1 hr, top with parchment paper. ◄
  9. Once chilled, preheat oven to 375°
  10. Line 2-3 large baking sheets with parchment paper.
    11. ► The # of batches will depend on how big/small you cut the cookies. ◄
  11. Remove one of the dough pieces from the refridgerator; cut into shapes.
  12. Move the cut cookies to baking sheet; re-roll remaining dough, repeat until gone.
  13. (Optional Step) Add sprinkles to dough prior to baking.
  14. Bake 8-11 minutes until very lightly colored on top and around edges.
    15. ► Rotate the baking sheet halfway through bake time. ◄
  15. Cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes; move to wire rack; cool completely prior to icing.
► For not so sweet cookies, you can add a little salt to the mix. ◄

Chocolate Oatmeal No-Bake Cookies
  • 2 Cups Sugar
  • ½ Cup Cocoa
  • ½ Cup Butter (1 Stick)
  • ½ Cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  • 3 Cups Quick-Cooking Oatmeal
  • ½ Cup Peanut Butter (Optional)
  1. Combine sugar, cocoa, butter, and milk in a saucepan.
  2. On medium heat, bring to a boil for 1 minute.
  3. Remove from heat; stir in peanut butter, vanilla, and oatmeal.
    4. ► The peanut butter is Optional, but gives a fantastic taste! ◄
  4. Drop by spoonful onto wax paper.
  5. Let cool for at least 30 minutes.

Simple Sugar Cookies
  • 2 Cups Sugar
  • 3 Eggs
  • 1 Cup Shortening
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 3 tsp Vanilla
  • 3 tsp Lemon Juice
  • 1 tbsp Salt
  • 3 tbsp Baking Powder
  • 5 Cups Flour
  1. Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. In a lg. bowl, mix together all ingredients.
  3. Roll dough out onto a floured surface.
  4. Cut out into desired shapes.
  5. Bake for about 8-10 minutes.
  6. Frost to your liking.

Power Cookies
  • 4 Cups Rolled Oats
  • 1 Can (15 Oz.) Cannellini Beans, Drained & Rinsed
  • ½ Cup Sugar
  • ½ Cup Brown Sugar
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  • 1 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1 tsp Baking Powder
  • 1 tsp Cinnamon
  • ½ Cup Dates, Pitted & Chopped
  • ½ Cup Flaked Coconut
  • ½ Cup Raisins
  • ½ Cup Walnuts, Chopped
  • ½ Cup Mango, Chopped
  1. Preheat oven to 325°.
  2. Grease cookie sheets; grind the oats in a blender, until resembling coarse flour.
  3. In a medium bowl, mash the beans to a smooth paste.
  4. Stir in the sugars, and vanilla, until well blended.
  5. Combine the ground oats, baking powder, baking soda, and cinnamon; blend into the bean mixture.
  6. Stir in the dates, coconut, raisins, and walnuts.
  7. Drop dough by heaping spoonfuls onto the prepared cookie sheet.
  8. Bake for 10-15 minues, until golden.
  9. Cool on baking sheets for 5 minutes; move to wire cooling racks to cool completely.

Soft Sugar Cookies
  • 1 Cup Butter
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • 3 Eggs
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  • 3½ Cups Flour
  • 2 tsp Cream of Tartar
  • 1 tsp Baking Soda
  • ½ tsp Salt
  1. Cream the butter and add the sugar gradually, beat until light and fluffy.
  2. Add eggs, one-at-a-time, mixing well after each addition.
  3. Stir in vanilla.
  4. Add flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt gradually to the cream mixture, stirring by hand.
  5. Cover dough and chill overnght.
  6. After chilling overnight, preheat oven to 375°.
  7. Roll dough out on a floured surface to ⅛"-¼" thick and cut into your desired shapes.
  8. Place cookies onto prepared baking sheets.
  9. Bake for 6-8 minutes, or until cookies have a golden appearance.

Chocolate Chip Wheat Cookies
  • 2 Cups Shortening
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • 4 Eggs
  • 2 tsp Vanilla
  • 2 tsp Baking Soda
  • 4½ Cups Whole Wheat Flour
  • 2 tsp Salt
  • 4 tsp Water
  • 1½ Cups Bropwn Sugar
  • 2 Cups Chocolate Chips
  1. Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. Blend shortening with brown sugar; add sugar and eggs.
  3. Add all other ingredients, except flour and mix well.
  4. Add flour, 1 cup at a time; add chocolate chips.
  5. Drop by spoonfuls on an ungreased cookie sheet.
  6. Bake for about 8-10 minutes.

Jelly-Filled Thumbprint Cookies
  • Non-Stick Cooking Spray
  • ½ Cup Brown Sugar, Packed
  • 1 Cup Shortening
  • 3 Lg. Eggs, Separated
  • 2 tbsp. Water
  • 1½ tsp. Vanilla
  • ¼ tsp. Salt
  • 2 Cups Flour
  • 2 Cups Pecans, Finely Chopped
  • 1 Cup Jelly, Any Flavor
  1. Preheat oven to 350°.
  2. Adjust the oven rack to the middle position; lightly coat 2 cookings sheets with the cooking spray.
  3. Beat brown sugar & shortening in a medium bowl with mixer at medium speed for about 2 minutes, or until fluffy.
  4. Scrape down the sides of the bowl; add yolks, water, vanilla, & salt, then beat until well blended.
  5. Add flour while mixer is going on low speed; contrinue beating until the mixture is well blended.
  6. Beat egg whites in a shallow bowl, until foamy; place pecans in a separate shallow bowl.
  7. Measure out about 2 tsp. of dough for each cookie; form into balls by rolling between palms.
  8. Dip each dough ball into the egg whites, then roll in pecans.
  9. Place dough balls onto a prepared cookie sheet.
  10. Using your thumb, make a rounded indent in the top of each cookie.
  11. Bake for about 10 minutes; remove from the oven.
  12. Place about 1 tsp. of the jelly into each indent of the cookie.
  13. Continue baking for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until lightly browned.
  14. Remove from oven & let cool for several minutes before serving.

Snickers®Gift Giving Cookies
  • ½ Cup Quick Oats
  • 2½ Cups Flour
  • ½ tsp. Baking Soda
  • 1 tsp. Salt
  • ½ Cup Butter, Softened
  • 1½ Cups Light Brown Sugar
  • 2 Eggs
  • 8 Regular-Sized Snickers® Bars, Chopped
  • 6 Plastic Treat Bags
  • 6 (18" ea) Strands Ribbon
  1. Preheat oven to 350°; line cookie sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a Lg. bowl, combine oats, flour, baking soda, and salt; set aside.
  3. In a 2nd bowl, beat the softened butter and brown sugar for about 3 minutes, or until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  5. Slowly add the oat mixture.
  6. Fold in the chopped Snickers® bars.
  7. Drop heaping tbsp. of the mixture onto the prepared cookie sheet at about 2" apart; slightly flatten the dough.
  8. Bake for about 10-12 minutes, or until golden brown.
  9. Once cookies have completely cooled, stack 5 and slip them into the treat bags.
  10. Tie the bags closed with the ribbon pieces.
