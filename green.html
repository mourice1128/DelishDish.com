Bacon & Cantaloupe Southwest Salad
  • 1 Pkg. "Southwest" Style Salad Kit
  • 5 Slices Bacon, Cooked & Cut Into 1" Pieces
  • 1 Cup Cantaloupe, Chopped
  • 2 tbsp Red Onion, Finely Chopped
  • Juice from ½ Lemon
  • 1 Ripe Avocado, Peeled & Chopped
  1. In a lg. bowl, combine all ingredients in salad kit, except the dressing kit that comes with the salad and the sour cream.
  2. Stir in bacon, cantaloupe, and onion.
  3. Toss lemon juice and avocado together, then add to the salad.
  4. Toss with salad dressing.
  5. Divide salad between 4 plates.
  6. Top each with a dollop of sour cream. (Optional)

Caesar Salad w/Grilled Beef, Tomato, & Mushroom Pesto Skewers
  • 8 (10") Wooden Skewers, Soaked in Water for 10 Minutes
  • 6 oz. Brown or White Button Mushrooms
  • 20 Lg. Basil Leaves
  • 24 Cherry Tomatoes
  • ⅓ Cup Bottled Pesto
  • 1 Lb. Top Sirloin Boneless Steak, Cut Into 1-1½" Chunks
  • Garlic Salt, As Needed
  • Lemon Pepper, As Needed
  • 1 Pkg. Caesar Salad Kit
  1. Thread, in this order:
    • 1 mushroom, through stem end.
    • 1 folded basil leaf.
    • 1 tomato.
    • Repeat 2 more times before ending with 2 tomatoes.
    • Repeat for a total of 4 skewers.
    • Brush vegetables with pesto.
  2. Thread in this order:
    • 4 pieces of steak onto each of the 4 remaining skewers.
    • Lightly sprinkle each side with garlic salt and lemon pepper.
  3. Grill vegetable and steak skewers over medium-high heat until vegetables are tender and steak is grilled to desired doneness.
  4. Serve with caesar salad kit.

Citrus Mahi Mahi w/Field Greens
  • 1½ Cups Water
  • ⅔ Cup Sugar
  • 6 tbsp Orange Juice Concentrate
  • ¼ Cup Honey
  • 1 tbsp Oilve Oil
  • 1 tbsp Grated Orange Peel
  • 1 tbsp Grated Lemon Peel
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 1 tsp Black Peppercorn, Crushed
  • 4 Mahi Mahi Fillets or Swordfish Steaks
  • 1 Cucumber
  • 1 Pkg. Field Greens
  • ¼ Cup Macadamia Nuts, Chopped, To Garnish
  1. In a medium skillet, stir together water, sugar, orange juice, honey, oil ,peels, lemon juice, & pepper.
  2. Heat to boiling, reduce heat & simmer for about 15 minutes, or until sause is reduced by ½, stirring occasionally.
  3. Cool & Strain.
  4. Remove about ⅓ cup for salad dressing.
  5. Pour remaining cooled liquid over fish in resealable plastic bag.
  6. Refridgerate and marinate for about 30 minutes.
  7. Cut cucumber in ½ lengthwise; scrape out all seeds.
  8. Cut each ½ into 2" pieces.
  9. Cut into matchstick slices & reserve for garnish.
  10. Remove fish from marinade; discard marinade.
  11. Grill or broil fish for 4-5 minutes, turn and continue cooking until deired doneness.
  12. Pour resevered dressing over salad; toss to evenly coat.
  13. Divide salad between 4 plates; top with fish.
  14. Sprinkle with nuts and garnish with cucumber.

Crunchy Pea Salad
  • 1 Pkg. (10 oz.) Frozen Petite Peas or 2 Cups Fresh Peas
  • 1 Cup Celery, Diced
  • 1 Cup Cauliflower, Chopped
  • 1 Cup Cashews, Chopped
  • ¾ Cup Green Onions
  • ¾ Cup Ranch Dressing
  • ¼ Cup Sour Cream
  • 2 tsp Dijion Mustard
  • 4 Strips Bacon, Cooked Crisp, Then Crumbled
  1. In a samll bowl, add the dresing, sour cream, and dijion mustard.
  2. Stir until well blended and set aside.
  3. Rinse peas in hot water, then drain.
  4. In a lg. bowl, combine the peas, celery, cauliflower, cashews, and onions.
  5. Toss the dressing together with the salad and chill covered for at least 1 hour.
  6. Sprinkle the bacon over the top and serve immediately.

Quick Potato Salad
  • 2 Lbs. Red Potatoes, Cooked & Cut Into 1" Cubes
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • ½ Cup Green Onions, Sliced
  • ¾ Cup Bacon Ranch Dressing
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Chives, Chopped
  • Dash of Salt & Pepper
  1. In a lg. pot, add potatoes and enough water to cover.
  2. Add the ¼ tsp salt and bring the water to a boil.
  3. Cook the potatoes for about 10-15 minutes or until tender.
  4. Drain and set aside to cool slightly.
  5. In a lg. bowl, mix the potatoes, green onions, and dressing until well coated.
  6. Chill covered for at least 1 hour.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste; garnish with chives.
