These will give you an idea for base cakes

Yellow Cake
  • 2⅓ Cups Flour
  • 1 tbsp Baking Powder
  • ¾ tsp Salt
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • ½ Cup Shortening
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  1. Pre-heat oven to 350°.
  2. Sift together flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  3. Cut in shortening until fine crumbs are formed.
  4. Add eggs, milk, and vanilla. Beat at low speed for 1 minute, then high for 2 minutes, scraping the bowl frequently.
  5. Pour into 9x13" pan and bake for 25-30 minutes.

White Cake
  • 2⅓ Cups Flour
  • 1 tbsp Baking Powder
  • ¾ tsp Salt
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • ½ Cup Shortening
  • 3 Egg Whites
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  1. Pre-heat oven to 350°.
  2. Sift together flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  3. Cut in shortening until fine crumbs are formed.
  4. Add egg whites, milk, and vanilla. Beat at low speed for 1 minute, then high for 2 minutes, scraping the bowl frequently.
    5. ► Beat egg whites separately for a lighter cake ◄
  5. Pour into 9x13" pan and bake for 25-30 minutes.

Chocolate Cake
  • 2⅓ Cups Flour
  • 1 tbsp Baking Powder
  • ¾ tsp Salt
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • ½ Cup Shortening
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  • ¼ Cup Cocoa Powder
  1. Pre-heat oven to 350°.
  2. Sift together flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  3. Cut in shortening until fine crumbs are formed.
  4. Add eggs, cocoa, milk, and vanilla. Beat at low speed for 1 minute, then high for 2 minutes, scraping the bowl frequently.
    5. ► Add cocoa prior to adding milk ◄
  5. Pour into 9x13" pan and bake for 25-30 minutes.

Spice Cake
  • 2⅓ Cups Flour
  • 1 tbsp Baking Powder
  • ¾ tsp Salt
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • ¼ tsp Ground Cloves
  • ¼ tsp Ground Allspice
  • ½ Cup Shortening
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  • 1 tsp Cinnamon
  1. Pre-heat oven to 350°.
  2. Sift together flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  3. Cut in shortening until fine crumbs are formed.
  4. Add eggs, ground cloves, ground allspice, cinnamon, milk, and vanilla. Beat at low speed for 1 minute, then high for 2 minutes, scraping the bowl frequently.
  5. Pour into 9x13" pan and bake for 25-30 minutes.

Chocolate Cake 2
  • 16 oz Semi-Sweet Chocolate
  • 1 Cup Unsalted Butter
  • 9 Eggs, Separated
  • 1 Cup Sugar
  • Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
  • or
  • Flour (For Dusting)
  1. Pre-heat oven to 350°.
  2. Butter a 9" Springform pan; line the bottom with buttered wax paper, then dust pan with either cocoa or flour.
  3. Slowly melt chocolate and butter together in a heavy saucepan over low heat, then allow to cool.
  4. Meanwhile, separate the eggs into large bowls. (One for egg whites, the other for the yolks)
  5. Beat the yolks for about 1 minute; slowly add sugar and continue beating until thick and lemon-colored.
  6. Beat egg whites until they just begin to peak.
  7. Add cooled chocolate mixture to egg yolks and blend thoroughly.
  8. Pour the chocolate and yolk mixture into egg whites and fold gently until completely blended.
  9. Remove ⅓ of the batter, cover, and then refridgerate.
  10. Pour the rest of the batter into prpared pan and bake for about 40-45 minutes.
  11. Cool for about ½ hour prior to turning out onto a serving plate.
  12. Frost with remaining batter.
  13. (Optional) Garnish with such items like:
    • Whipped Cream
    • Sliced Almonds
    • Powdered Sugar

Texas Sheet Cake
  • 2 Cups Flour
  • 2 Cups Sugar
  • ½ Cup Buttermilk
  • 1 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1 Cup Water
  • 1 Stick Butter
  • ½ Shortening
  • 3-4 tbsp Baking Cocoa
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  • 2 Eggs
  1. Pre-heat oven to 400°.
  2. Mix flour and sugar in a mixing bowl.
  3. In a saucepan, bring to boil the water, butter, shortening, and cocoa.
  4. Stir baking soda into buttermilk; add to mixing bowl along with the cocoa mix.
  5. Mix well; add eggs and beat for a few minutes.
  6. Pour onto greased and floured cookie sheet.
  7. Bake for 20 minutes.

Prune Cake
  • 1 Cup Chopped Prunes
  • 1 Cup Sugar
  • 1 tsp Cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp Allspice
  • 3 tsp Baking Powder
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • ½ Cup Milk
  • ½ Cup Butter
  • 2 Eggs
  • ¼ tsp Nutmeg
  • 2 Cups Flour
  • ¼ tsp Baking Soda
  • ¼ Cup Prune Juice
  • 1 Cup Chopped Walnuts (optional)
  1. Pre-heat oven to 350°.
  2. Cook and chop prunes prior to measuring.
  3. Cream butter, sugar, and spices until light and fluffy.
  4. Add eggs, one-at-a-time, beating well after each addition.
  5. Sift flour, then measure, then sift again along with baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  6. Add dry ingredients alternately with prune juice and milk to creamed mixture.
  7. Stir in prunes and walnuts.
  8. Pour into 2 greased and floured 8" layer cake pans (lined with paper).
  9. Bake for 30 minutes or until cake tests done.
  10. Remove from pan and cool on a rack.
  11. Serve plain or frosted or warm with tart lemmon or orange sauce.
