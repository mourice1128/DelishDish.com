Baked Turkey and Cheese Sandwiches
  • 1 tbsp Butter, Softened
  • 8 Slices of Bread (Depending on taste desired)
  • 8 Slices of Deli Turkey
  • 4 Slices of cheese (Depending on taste desired)
  • 2 cans Tomato Soup (Optional)
  1. Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. Line baking sheet with foil.
  3. Lightly butter 1 side of 4 of the slices of bread.
  4. Place bread, butter side down, on the baking sheet.
  5. Top each of the slices with 2 slices of turkey and 1 slice of cheese, followed by the remainin slices of bread.
  6. Butter the tops of the bread.
  7. Bake 10-12 minutes, turning once halfway through, until bread is golden brown.
  8. SLice in quarters and serve with tomato soup. (Optional)

Cresent Dogs with Dipping Sauce
  • 1 8oz. pkg. of Refridgerated Cresent Rolls
  • 8 Hot Dogs
  • 2-4 oz. Deli Cheddar Cheese Slices, Cut Into Strips
  • ½ Cup Canned RO*TEL Diced Tomatoes and Green Chilies
  • ½ Cup Ranch Dressing
  1. Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. Unroll dough and separate.
  3. Roll each portion into a 12" rope and wrap in a spiral around each hot dog.
  4. Arrange on a foil-lined baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes.
  6. Top with cheese.
  7. Bake an additional 1-2 minutes, until the cheese is melted.
  8. Combine RO*TEL and ranch dressing, then serve.

Cheesy Chicken Quesadilla
  • 4 oz. Quick-Melting Cheese, Cubed
  • 1 Cup Cream Cheese
  • 1 Cup Salsa
  • 3 tbsp Green Onions, Sliced
  • 12 oz. Cooked Chicken Breast, Diced
  • 8-8" Tortillas
  • ½ Cup Sour Cream
  1. In a small mixing bowl, mix together ½ cup of salsa with the cream cheese.
  2. Spread salsa mixture on 4 tortillas, then sprinkle with remaining ingredients, excluding salsa and sour cream.
  3. Cover with remaining tortillas.
  4. Spray a lg. skillet with cooking spray.
  5. Cook quesadillas on medium-high heat for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until crisp and lightly browned.
  6. Cut into wedges and garnish with sour cream and remaining salsa.

BBQ Beef Brisket Sandwiches w/Coleslaw
  • 1 Red Onion, Divided
  • 2 Cups Green Cabbage, Shredded
  • 2 Cups Red Cabbage, Shredded
  • ¼ Mayonnaise or Whipped Salad Dressing
  • 1 Bottle (18 oz.) BBQ Sauce, Divided
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable Oil
  • 1 Brisket (3 Lb.) Beef, Trimmed
  • 1 Can (14.5 oz.) Beef Broth
  • 2 Cloves Garlic, Minced
  • 12 Kaiser Rolls
  1. Preheat oven to 350°.
  2. Cut ½ the onion into thin slices; place in a lg. bowl.
  3. Add cabbage; mix lightly.
  4. Mix dressing and 2 tbsp BBQ sauce.
  5. Add to cabbage mixture; toss to coat.
  6. Refridgerate until ready to use.
  7. Heat oill in a lg. skillet; add meat and cook for 5 minutes, or until browned on both sides; turn after 3 minutes.
  8. Transfer to a 13x9" baking dish.
  9. Chop remaining onion piece; sprinkle over meat.
  10. Mix broth, ½ cup of the remaining BBQ sauce, and garlic; pour over meat and cover.
  11. Bake for about 2½-3 hours or until meat is done.
  12. Let stand covered for about 10 minutes.
  13. Shred meat and place into a lg. bowl.
  14. Add remaining BBQ suace and toss to coat.
  15. Fill each roll with ½ cup meat mixture and ⅓ cup coleslaw just before serving.

Potatoes & Cheese Bruncheros
  • 9 Rhodes™ Dinner Rolls or 6 Rhodes Texas™ Rolls, Thawed
  • 4 Cups Hashbrwon Potatoes, Shredded
  • 1 Onion, Chopped
  • ¼ Cup Butter
  • Salt & Pepper taste
  • 1 Can (4 oz.) Green Chiles, Chopped
  • ½ Cup Sour Cream
  • 10 Eggs, Well Beaten
  • 1½ Cups Cheddar Cheese, Shredded
  • ¼ Cup Cilantro, Chopped
  • Taco Sauce, Salsa, and/or Sour Cream (Optional)
  1. Preheat oven to 350°.
  2. Spray prep area with non-sick cooking spray.
  3. Combine rolls together and roll into a 13" circle or 10x14" rectangle.
  4. Place in sprayed 12" deep-dish pizza pan or 9x13" pan and cover with sprayed plastic wrap.
  5. Let rise for 15 Minutes.
  6. Remove wrap and poke with fork several times to prevent bubbles from forming.
  7. Bake for 12 minutes.
  8. While crust is baking, and in a lg. frying pan, brown the potatoes and onions in butter.
  9. Salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Spread chiles and sour cream on top of the potatoes and mix together.
  11. In a separate fying pan, scramble eggs until just barely set.
  12. Salt and pepper to taste.
  13. Layer eggs on crust, followed by the potato mixture.
  14. Sprinkle the cheese and cilantro on top.
  15. Bake for about 10 more minutes.
  16. Serve warm with taco sauce, salsa, and/or sour cream, if desired.

Chili in Tortilla Bowls
  • 1½ Lbs. Ground Beef
  • 1 Onion, Chopped
  • 6 Burrito-Size Tortillas
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper, Diced
  • 2 tbsp Chili Powder
  • 1 Can (19 oz.) Red Kidney Beans, Rinsed & Drained
  • 1 Can (11 oz.) Whole Kernal Corn, Drained
  • 1 Jar (1 Lb. 8 oz.) Chunky Pasta Sauce
  • 1 Cup Cheddar Cheese, Shredded
  1. In a 12" skillet, brown ground beef over medium-high heat; stirring occasionally.
  2. Add onion, red pepper, and chili powder; cook for about 5 minutes or until the onion is tender, while still stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in beans, corn, and pasta sauce.
  4. Bring to a boil over high heat; reduse heat to low and simmer covered for about 20 minutes, while still stirring ocasionally.
  5. Meanwhile, using one tortilla at a time, press into a 1-2 cup microwave-safe bowl to form a bowl shape.
  6. Microwave on high for 1½ minutes.
  7. Let cool for 1 minute.
  8. Gently lift out and arrange on a serving plate; repeat with each remaining tortillas.
  9. To serve, spoon chili into tortilla bowls, then sprinkle with cheese.

Budweiser® BBQ Chicken Pizza
For the crust:

  • 1¾-2¼ Cups Flour
  • 1 pkg. Fleischmann's® Pizza Crust Yeast
  • 1½ tsp Sugar
  • ¾ tsp Salt
  • ⅔ Cup Very Warm Water
  • 3 tbsp Olive Oil
Toppings:

  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 2 Chicken Thighs, Boneless & Skinless
  • ½ Cup Budweiser® BBQ sauce
  • ⅛ Cup Onions, Chopped
  • 1 Cup Mozzarella Cheese
  • 1 Cup Shredded Cheddar Cheese
  • 2 tbsp Cilantro, Chopped
  1. Preheat oven to 425°.
  2. Combine 1 cup of the flour, undisolved yeast, sugar, and salt in a lg. bowl.
  3. Add very warm water and 3 tbsp of the olive oil (leaving the 1 tbsp for step #9).
  4. Bring to a boil over high heat; reduse heat to low and simmer covered for about 20 minutes, while still stirring ocasionally; mix until well blended.
  5. Gradually add more of the flour until the dough is soft; Dough should form a ball and be slighty sticky.
  6. Knead on a floured surface, adding additional flour as needed, until smooth and elastic.
  7. Pat dough with floured hands to fill greased pizza pan or baking sheet.
  8. Form a rim by pinching the edge of the dough.
  9. Heat the remaining tbsp of olive oilin a skillet over medium heat; add chicken and cookm for 5 minutes on each side.
  10. Remove the chicken and place onto a cutting board and let rest for 5 minutes.
  11. Cut into bit-size pieces; spread BBQ sauce over crust and top with onions and cheese, then with chicken.
  12. Bake on lowest oven rack for 12-15 minutes, until cheese is bubly and crust is browned.
  13. Sprinkle with cilantro prioe to serving.
